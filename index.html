Dear Sir, 


I hope you are doing well. I am writing to follow up on the concerning matter of my ward, Mr. Rohit Gandaas, which was discussed with you verbally around 10 days ago. The issue pertains to the handling of Mr. Gandaas' case by the university authorities.


 Allow me to provide some context. Due to being out of town, I requested my ward to meet with you and explain his concerns. During the meeting, he only shared the information that was already documented, without mentioning the names of the authorities he interacted with.


To shed light on the pertinent individuals, I wish to bring to your attention the name of "Mr. M.P. Aggarwal," who holds the position of Chief Finance and Accounting Officer. The manner in which Mr. Aggarwal communicated with my ward during their interactions has been distressing. I feel it is crucial to highlight some of the alarming statements made by Mr. Aggarwal: 
• "We will spoil your degree, and none of us will be affected except you." 
• "If you want to take legal action, take it."
• "VC won't decide your admission, I will." (He even made a threatening remark, saying "should I call the VC and tell him to terminate your admission? He will.") 
• "I won't unblock your amizone account; go wherever you were before or where you want to take admission. I am not accepting your admission." (He also threatened to erase the 5th-semester result from the records as if it never existed.)  And etc.


The rest of the issues have already been mentioned in the documents submitted to you and conveyed verbally by my ward. To reiterate and highlight the major points, The email communication that followed raised distressing issues concerning fee structures, penalty decisions, account blockage, and examination participation. Additionally, Mr. Rohit Gandaas was pressured into writing an email expressing his willingness to change his program from B.Tech CSE INTL to B.Tech CSE, with the possibility of paying international fees and a hefty penalty. However, the university management has not provided clear explanations for imposing a 2 lakh penalty on Mr. Rohit Gandaas, leading to further questions and concerns. Even after paying the forced penalty of 2 lakh rupees, which was presented as the only option to sit in the examination, my ward's account remained blocked.


I urgently request your intervention in resolving this situation promptly. It is crucial for the university to reconsider the imposed penalty and provide a written response addressing all concerns. Furthermore, I kindly request a thorough investigation into the conduct of the authorities involved to prevent such unprofessional behavior in the future.


This case has been ongoing for a year now, and with the recent declaration of my ward's results and the deadline for the 7th-semester fee payment being 17th July, I implore you to address this matter on an urgent basis.


The well-being of our students should be of utmost importance, and I trust that you will take the necessary actions to uphold their rights and address their concerns.


I eagerly await your immediate response and cooperation.


Sincerely,
